Robert D. Levin Overview and Wiki

Early Education and Career

Levin, born on October 13, 1947, in Brooklyn, New York, was drawn to music from a young age.

He attended the Brooklyn Friends School and Andrew Jackson High School before spending a year in Paris under the guidance of the legendary Nadia Boulanger.

Later, at Harvard University, he earned a Bachelor of Arts magna cum laude in 1968. His thesis, The Unfinished Works of W. A. Mozart, was a precursor to his lifelong passion for reconstructing Mozart’s compositions.

After graduation, he joined the Curtis Institute of Music as head of its theory department. Over the years, he also served as:

  • Coordinator of theory instruction at SUNY Purchase
  • Professor of Piano at the Hochschule für Musik Freiburg
  • Dwight P. Robinson Jr. Professor of the Humanities at Harvard University

In these roles, he taught musicology, classical keyboard instruments, and conducting.

Musical Contributions

One of Levin’s crowning achievements is his reconstruction of the Mozart Requiem in D minor. He also completed the Great Mass in C minor, staying faithful to Mozart’s original style.

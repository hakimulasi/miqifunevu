Inception

Microsoft has made huge steps since its inception by the inventive minds of Paul Allen and Bill Gates. The two started off as very good friends considering that they grew up together. The pair had some computer skills and decided to start a business using their skills. They started off with Traf-O-Data, which sold rudimentary computers to track and analyze automobile traffic data.

Bill Gates headed off to Harvard University while Paul Allen went to Washington State University. Paul dropped out of university to work in Honeywell. One day, he read an article in the newspaper concerning Micro Instrumentation and Telemetry Systems (MITS), which inspired him to create an interpreter for the device.

The two worked on the device and demonstrated it to MITS, which they did flawlessly. They later started Microsoft on April 4, 1975, with Bill Gates as the CEO. In August 1977, the company had an agreement with ASCII Microsoft, which led them to have their first international office in Japan. Microsoft entered the operating system business in 1980 with its own version of Unix called Xenix. They received a contract from IBM for them to provide their own version of CP/M OS to be used on IBM/PC (Personal Computer). It was a successful deal that led Microsoft to retain ownership of MS-DOS.

Microsoft eventually became the leading PC operating systems vendor. The company later expanded into other such the release of the Microsoft Mouse in 1983 and the publishing division of Microsoft press. Later on, Allen retired from Microsoft because he suffered from Hodgkin’s disease. Apparently, Gates wanted to dilute his share of the company’s shares because he did not think he was working hard enough.

This statement was stated by Paul Allen himself in the Idea Man; A Memoir by the co-founder of Microsoft. Allen later invested in low-tech sectors, sports teams, commercial real estate, neuroscience, private space flight, and many others. Microsoft then continued to flourish under the guidance of Bill Gates. It flourished as a software developing company that also dealt with dealing with electronic devices.

Evolution

At this point, Microsoft was already a very big company. Their inventions spurred a lot of success for them. Microsoft began inventing Microsoft Windows, which was a big break for the company. Over the years, Microsoft has been known to make around four billionaires and around 12,000 millionaires. This demonstrates the success of this company.

They also ventured in other areas apart from just making computers. They’ve made phones and also gaming consoles, which has brought them a lot of success. Microsoft has made it to the Fortune 500 rankings as number 30 with many shareholders and investors with a big interest in the company.

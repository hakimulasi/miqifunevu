Sudden Tremors Rock Punjab and Haryana Today, on June 13, a powerful earthquake with a magnitude of 6.6 on the Richter scale shook Chandigarh, the capital of Punjab and Haryana, along with surrounding areas like Mohali. This unexpected event occurred at 10:20 PM on Thursday night, leaving people startled and frightened.

Source of the Tremor The earthquake’s epicenter was located 133 kilometers south of Fayzabad in Afghanistan, according to the National Centre for Seismology. The impact of the quake was felt not only in Chandigarh but also in neighboring regions like Delhi NCR, Jammu-Kashmir, and Rajasthan. Even Islamabad, the capital of Pakistan, experienced strong tremors, along with various other cities in the country.

Past Earthquake Tragedy In 2005, a devastating earthquake with a magnitude of 7.5 claimed the lives of over 74,000 people in Pakistan. This tragic event serves as a grim reminder of the destructive power of such natural disasters. The recent tremors also jolted parts of north India, including Delhi-NCR.

Accounts of Witnesses Residents shared their experiences of the earthquake. A Noida resident described feeling the dining table shake, followed by the fans. The intensity and duration of the quake were notable. In Delhi, a car owner near Connaught Place, named Ramesh Pawar, recounted how his vehicle began shaking while he was waiting for passengers. Startled, he quickly alerted his friends.

Swift Response to the Emergency As the news spread, panic-stricken individuals hurried out of buildings, seeking safety from the tremors. Fortunately, there have been no reports of significant property damage so far. Authorities are closely monitoring the situation and are ready to provide assistance if needed.
